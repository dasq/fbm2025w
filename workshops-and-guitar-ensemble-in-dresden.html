Junior Ensemble

  • Pop & Folk Songs

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know

What level do I need to join an ensemble?

Our ensembles welcome all levels. For beginners, we have special arrangements with simpler parts that still let you contribute meaningfully to the group sound.

Do I need to read music?

While reading helps, it's not required. We provide audio examples and learn many pieces by ear. Our arrangements include tablature as well as standard notation.

Are performances mandatory?

Performances are encouraged but optional. We understand stage fright is real! That said, most members find our informal concerts rewarding and not stressful.

What styles of music do you play?

We focus on accessible arrangements of pop, jazz, folk and classical pieces. Recent repertoire includes everything from Christmas carols to Michael Jackson hits.

How large are the groups?

We keep ensembles small (4-8 players) to ensure everyone gets attention. Multiple groups run simultaneously if there's more interest.

Can I try before committing?

Absolutely! We offer trial sessions so you can experience the ensemble dynamic before making a longer commitment.

Do I need my own guitar?

Yes, please bring your own instrument. We can advise on suitable guitars if you're looking to purchase.

How often do workshops occur?

We plan 3-4 special workshops per year, typically tied to projects like our Christmas recording or preparing for a concert.

Can parents observe?

For junior ensembles, parents are welcome to observe occasional sessions, though we find children often focus better without an audience.

What's the age range for adult ensemble?

Our adult members range from university students to retirees. The common factor is enthusiasm for making music together!

Do you provide the music?

Yes, all arrangements are provided. Many are custom-made by Farid specifically for our ensembles.

How do I join?

Simply book a trial session via the website or contact us to discuss which group might suit you best.
